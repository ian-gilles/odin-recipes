Butter Bean Pottage

Description

Pottage is simply a thick vegetable stew that optionally contains grains or meat. It is one of those unique meals that was served equally to peasants, knights, and kings depending on the quality of ingredients and presentation. As suggested by the name, pottage is traditionally cooked and served all within one pot. This recipe would likely have been served to low nobility or peasantry on a special occassion due to containing bacon.

For more information about pottage and the medieval history behind it, check out its Wikipedia page here.

Ingredients

Serves 6

  • 8 slices of thick-cut bacon
  • 3 leeks (or 2 and half an onion)
  • 1 stalk of celery
  • 4 sprigs of thyme
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 3 8 oz. cans of butter beans or other white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 t whole-grain or stone-ground mustard
  • 600 ml vegetable broth
  • 200 ml water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Using a knife or scissors, cut bacon into bite-size pieces.
  2. Cut leek in half length-wise. Fan the separate segments under cool water to wash away any remaining alluvium. Slice into ~0.5cm sections.
  3. Taking a knife, slice the celery concave up along the start of the curves so that three equal size strips remain. Roughly chop into small pieces.
  4. Add the bacon to a large, pre-heated pot and let cook for about 5 minutes.
  5. Add your veggies and thyme, but not your garlic. Cook over low for 15 minutes.
  6. While this cooks, mince your garlic. When about 30 seconds remain, add your garlic and cook until fragrant.
  7. Add remaining ingredients, bring to a boil, then simmer with lid for about 4 minutes.
  8. At this point, begin mashing or blending with an immersion blender the pottage.
  9. Take off heat and serve with a dark, crusty bread or some other carb.
